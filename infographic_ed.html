Goal: Akshardham

Ultimate Liberation
BHAKTI
4. Bhakti (Devotion) Analogy: The Super Magnet Engine

Just as a magnet sticks to iron, Bhakti is when the mind sticks to God's Murti like "a pebble sticks to butter."

Definition: Intense affection for God combined with knowledge of His greatness. Believing oneself to be Aksharrup.

VAIRAGYA
3. Vairagya (Detachment) Analogy: The Non-Stick Coating

A coating that prevents space debris (worldly desires) from sticking to the ship. Even if you have nice things, you don't care about them.

Definition: Having no attachment to anything created (Maya). Keeping the mind constantly focused only on God.

GNAN
2. Gnan (Knowledge) Analogy: X-Ray Glasses & Map

Knowing the difference between the ship (body) and the pilot (soul), like a "sword and its sheath."

Definition: Realizing one's true self as Atma, distinct from the body and Gunas. Identifying with Aksharbrahma.

DHARMA
1. Dharma (Righteousness) Analogy: Steering Wheel & Navigation

Keeps the ship on the correct path. Without it, you crash.

Definition: Following God's commands (non-violence, celibacy, panch vartaman). No bad thoughts arising in the mind.

Mahatmya (Greatness) Analogy: The Infinite Power Fuel

Without this fuel, the engine (Bhakti) stops working. It makes faith and trust strong.

Definition: The profound understanding of God's immeasurable greatness. Realizing God is infinitely great.

The Aksharbrahma Satpurush

You cannot build this spaceship alone.

The Satpurush is the Master Mechanic who helps you install Dharma, Gnan, Vairagya, and Bhakti.

"Such an Ekantik Dharma is attained only through the association of such an Ekantik Purush."

How to build it?

  • Associate with the Satpurush (Prasang).
  • He gives the strength to imbibe these qualities.
  • He connects you to the command of Shriji Maharaj.
