Sagun & Nirgun

Understanding the Nature of Parabrahma Purushottam Narayan

"Sagun-Nirgunpanu Haribhaktne janyu joie, nikar to gotha khay"
(A devotee must understand the sagun and nirgun aspects of God, otherwise they will be misled) - Shriji Maharaj

A Divine Harmony

In the BAPS Swaminarayan tradition, these terms are not contradictions.
"Parabrahma's sagun-ness and nirgun-ness are both real, natural, ultimate, and eternal." They represent the divine opulence (alaukik aishwarya) of God.

Subtle & Pure

1. Based on Qualities:

"Extremely Subtle Form" (atyant sukshma swarup). Also known as the Anvay form. Like the sky or soul, He pervades all yet remains unattached.

3. The Gold Analogy (Application & Conviction)

Sagun (The Ornament) The intricate form (Human-like) possessing value, beauty, and artistry (Kalyankari guno). Nirgun (The Element) The devotee uses the "X-ray of Faith" to see that despite the form, it is 100% pure gold, flawless and untarnished by base metals (Maya).
